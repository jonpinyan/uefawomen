Russia is presently banned from play in all UEFA competitions.
For the 2024-25 season, Russia would have been entitled to one berth in the UWCL, with the champion entering in round C1.
For the 2025-26 season, if re-instated, Russia will be entitled to two berths in European play. The champion will enter the UWCL in Round C2, and the runner-up will enter the Europa Cup in the first round.
For the 2026-27 season, if re-instated, Russia will be entitled to one berth in the UWCL, with the champion entering in Round C1.

League Format

Russia's league is Women's Football League, which plays on a summer schedule. Thirteen teams play a double round-robin, for a total of 24 games in 26 matchdays. The 2024 season has concluded, with Zenit St. Petersburg (champions) and CSKA Moscow (runner-up) finishing in what would be qualifying places for European play.
