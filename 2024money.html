Prize Money Distribution for the 2023-24 UEFA Women's Champions League

This page lists the prize money received for each team in the 2023-24 UEFA Women's Champions League. In each of the four stages, money is distributed via a different system.

  • In Round 1, the home team receives €30,000, while each visiting team receives €65,000. In addition, the first place team receives €15,000, second place €10,000, third place €5,000, and fourth place €1,000.
  • In Round 2, the losing team receives €140,000, while the winning team receives €100,000.
  • For qualifying for the group stage, each team receives €400,000. In addition, each team receives €50,000 for each win and €17,000 for each draw. Finally, the team that wins each group receives a €20,000 bonus.
  • In the knockout stage, teams earn an increasing amount for each round reached. Quarterfinalists receive €160,000. Semifinalists receive €180,000. The runner-up receives €200,000 and the champion receives €350,000. These amounts are cumulative, so the champion receives a total of €690,000 for the knockout stage.
There is some money that is undistributed, because some associations do not participate (thus meaning there is no preliminary round, and some of the Round 1 visitor stipends are not needed), and because of group stage draws. UEFA distributes these funds to the 25 teams with the greatest travel distance for Rounds 1 and 2, but the specific details of that distribution are not publicly announced, so those funds are not catalogued here.
